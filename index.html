My name is Angel Chen. I am a Political Science/Public Service major. I plan on attending law school upon graduation from undergraduate school. In the future I will become a political commentator with a law degree. I believe law is the foundation of all causes. In the past I was politically engaged with Junior States of America, an all student run, non-partisan and non-profit organization, where students from all over the Southern California JSA States come together to debate about the current political, social and international problems. As the Convention Coordinator I helped put up conventions for more than 1,000 SoCal high schoolers, from hotel registration to coordinating all blocks of events, from guiding attendees, to making sure everyone feels comfortable. As a SoCal Cabinet Member, it helped me develop my leadership skills. It helped me become more confident in public speaking, capable of active listening and developing critical thinking skills.

While I was part of the Junior States of America, I was also actively engaged with a local campaign of Bill Essayli for Assembly back in 2018 Midterm election. During this time, we phone banked, we had voter activism and we participated in multiple parades. It really gave me a reason to step out of my comfort zone, to engage with the older generation, actively listening to their concerns and helping them find a solution.
